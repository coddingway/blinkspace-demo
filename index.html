Context

Why BlinkSpace Exists

The space economy is undergoing a structural shift. What that means for companies trying to raise capital, enter new markets, and find strategic partners is not obvious — until you have been inside it.

India's liberalisation through IN-SPACe, Europe's push for strategic autonomy in space, the US commercial space economy's maturation, and the rapid emergence of space programmes across the Indo-Pacific and Gulf regions are reshaping where capital flows, where technology originates, and where markets open.

Simultaneously, dual-use technologies — from Earth observation and satellite communications to positioning, navigation, and advanced materials — now serve commercial, institutional, and national security markets through the same value chains.

"Companies that understand this convergence will define the next decade of the space economy. Those that do not will be defined by it."
